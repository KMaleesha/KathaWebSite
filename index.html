Project Insight

Speech and language development are critical aspects of a child's overall well-being, closely linked to their educational achievements, social interactions, and personality development. However, in Sri Lanka—where over 16 million people speak Sinhala—there is a significant gap in diagnosing and treating phonological disorders among young children, especially in rural and low-resource settings. Traditional assessment methods are manual and carried out by speech pathologists, who are often in short supply and inaccessible in these regions

To address this challenge, our research introduces "Katha," a groundbreaking mobile application specifically designed for Sinhala-speaking children aged 2 to 4. This app employs machine learning and neural networks across four main features: the Speech Accuracy Detection Unit for real-time pronunciation feedback, the Phonological Disorder Detection Unit for comprehensive speech analysis and diagnostic reports, the Sinhala Speech Transcription Unit for automated speech-to-text conversion, and the Therapeutic Game Unit that uses human-computer interaction principles to create engaging therapeutic exercises. These features work in synergy to make speech pathology services more affordable, accessible, and efficient, especially in underserved areas.

The impact of Katha extends beyond technological innovation; it aims for a societal transformation. By ensuring early detection and intervention, the app helps mitigate the long-term effects of untreated phonological disorders. It empowers parents and caregivers with actionable insights and recommended therapeutic activities, enabling them to be proactive in their child's speech development journey. Additionally, the app provides invaluable support to healthcare professionals by offering up-to-date patient data, facilitating targeted and effective treatment plans. In summary, Katha has the potential to revolutionize the landscape of speech and language disorder care in Sri Lanka by leveraging advanced technologies for timely, cost-effective, and accessible solutions.

Approach

Speech Accuracy Detection

The cornerstone of the "Katha" app is its advanced speech accuracy detection unit. Utilizing machine learning techniques like Support Vector Machine (SVM), this feature plays a crucial role in real-time speech analysis. Children are encouraged to pronounce words displayed through visual cues, which are then recorded and processed for accuracy. The feature has an impressive accuracy rate of approximately 88.92%, offering immediate visual feedback to help the young user understand the correctness of their pronunciation. This powerful tool goes beyond mere detection and serves as an interactive learning experience, setting a new standard in the realm of speech development apps.

Sinhala Speech Transcription

This feature focuses on the seamless conversion of spoken words into written text. Designed to cater to children aged 2-4 years, the speech-to-text model is created to capture both accurately pronounced and mispronounced words. Deploying Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) layers, the model shows an exceptional transcription accuracy of 90.06%. This transcription is integral not only for detecting phonological errors but also for generating insightful reports that assist speech pathologists in providing comprehensive patient care.

Phonological Disorder Detection

Perhaps one of the most innovative aspects of the app is its ability to identify potential phonological disorders in children. Using another LSTM-based machine learning model, this feature pinpoints errors and assigns a severity score to each mispronunciation. It boasts an accuracy of 87.76% in identifying and categorizing mispronunciations, providing invaluable data for monitoring a child's speech development over time. This module is a significant advancement in early detection and intervention for speech-related disorders among children.

Therapeutic Gaming Activities

Recognizing the importance of engagement in learning, the app incorporates game-based educational interventions specifically tailored for its young audience. The activities are designed using Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) techniques and are devoid of written words to accommodate the reading limitations of the target age group. Instead, they rely on vibrant visuals and sensory experiences to maintain the child’s interest. The app also features a child narrator named "Pancha" who serves as a friendly guide, making the gaming experience both educational and enjoyable. This unique blend of gaming and learning offers a promising approach to early language acquisition.

Research Problem

1. How can employee satisfaction levels be accurately predicted?

2. How to detect employee emotions and identify motivation level?

3. How to monitor the applications that employees use?

4. How to monitor the website usage?

Research Objectives

1. If employee performance falls short of expectations, employers may arrange for additional training, workshops, and awareness sessions. Businesses can learn a lot about their employees' efficiency and production.

2. Determining employee motivation levels utilizing recognized human emotions is critical to attaining the primary goal of ensuring job satisfaction. First, use an image collecting tool to take images at regular intervals that can be imported for processing and analysis. The next stage is to create reports that include classified emotions.

3. Monitoring the applications that employees use, and make a list of how those apps are used. The technology tracks employees' productivity based on the amount of time they spend on their computers and online.

4. Detailed website usage data is presented. This part monitors by tracking websites visited, URLs, check uploads and downloads of used websites, content kinds, and efficient versus unproductive browsing.
